MONETARY AUTHORITY OF SINGAPORE

MAS NOTICE 648
PREVENTION OF MONEY LAUNDERING AND COUNTERING THE FINANCING OF TERRORISM
— CAPITAL MARKETS INTERMEDIARIES

Effective Date: 1 April 2020

SECTION 3 — CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE

3.1 General Requirements

A capital markets intermediary ("CMI") shall conduct customer due diligence
("CDD") measures when:
(a) establishing business relations with any customer;
(b) carrying out any transaction exceeding SGD 20,000;
(c) there is a suspicion of money laundering or terrorism financing;
(d) the CMI has doubts about the veracity of any information previously obtained.

3.2 Enhanced Due Diligence

3.2.1 A CMI shall perform enhanced due diligence ("EDD") for:
(a) Politically Exposed Persons ("PEPs");
(b) Correspondent banking relationships with institutions from jurisdictions
    with strategic AML/CFT deficiencies.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

SECTION 4 — SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING

4.1 Reporting Obligations

4.1.1 Where a CMI has reasonable grounds to suspect that any transaction
is related to money laundering or terrorism financing, the CMI shall file
a Suspicious Transaction Report ("STR") with the Suspicious Transaction
Reporting Office within 15 business days from the date of detection.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

SECTION 5 — RECORD KEEPING

5.1 CDD Records

5.1.1 A CMI shall maintain all CDD records for a minimum period of 5 years
after:
(a) the date of termination of the business relationship; or
(b) the date of completion of the transaction (for one-off transactions).

This Notice shall take effect on 1 April 2020.
