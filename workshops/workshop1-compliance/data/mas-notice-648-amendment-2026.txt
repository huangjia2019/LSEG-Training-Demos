MONETARY AUTHORITY OF SINGAPORE

MAS NOTICE 648 (AMENDMENT NO. 3)
PREVENTION OF MONEY LAUNDERING AND COUNTERING THE FINANCING OF TERRORISM
— CAPITAL MARKETS INTERMEDIARIES

Effective Date: 1 July 2026
Published: 15 March 2026

SECTION 3 — CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE

3.1 General Requirements

A capital markets intermediary ("CMI") shall conduct customer due diligence
("CDD") measures when:
(a) establishing business relations with any customer;
(b) carrying out any transaction exceeding SGD 5,000 (amended from SGD 20,000);
(c) there is a suspicion of money laundering or terrorism financing;
(d) the CMI has doubts about the veracity of any information previously obtained.

3.2 Enhanced Due Diligence

3.2.1 A CMI shall perform enhanced due diligence ("EDD") for:
(a) Politically Exposed Persons ("PEPs") from jurisdictions rated "high-risk"
    by the Financial Action Task Force ("FATF");
(b) All new corporate accounts where beneficial owners are from jurisdictions
    identified as non-cooperative by FATF;
(c) Any transaction involving virtual assets or virtual asset service providers
    exceeding SGD 1,000 (NEW);
(d) Correspondent banking relationships with institutions from jurisdictions
    with strategic AML/CFT deficiencies.

3.2.2 EDD measures shall include, but not be limited to:
(a) Obtaining senior management approval before establishing the business
    relationship;
(b) Taking reasonable measures to establish the source of wealth and source
    of funds;
(c) Conducting enhanced ongoing monitoring of the business relationship.

SECTION 4 — SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING

4.1 Reporting Obligations

4.1.1 Where a CMI has reasonable grounds to suspect that any transaction
is related to money laundering or terrorism financing, the CMI shall file
a Suspicious Transaction Report ("STR") with the Suspicious Transaction
Reporting Office within 5 business days (amended from 15 business days)
from the date of detection.

4.1.2 A CMI shall implement automated transaction monitoring systems
capable of real-time screening by 1 January 2027 (NEW). Such systems shall:
(a) Screen all transactions against the latest sanctions lists within 24 hours
    of list updates;
(b) Generate alerts for transactions matching suspicious patterns;
(c) Maintain an audit trail of all screening decisions.

4.1.3 Where a CMI files an STR, the CMI shall not inform the customer
or any other person about the filing.

SECTION 5 — RECORD KEEPING

5.1 CDD Records

5.1.1 A CMI shall maintain all CDD records for a minimum period of 7 years
(amended from 5 years) after:
(a) the date of termination of the business relationship; or
(b) the date of completion of the transaction (for one-off transactions).

5.2 Transaction Records

5.2.1 A CMI shall maintain records of all transactions for a minimum period
of 7 years after the date of the transaction.

5.3 STR Records

5.3.1 A CMI shall maintain records related to STRs for a minimum period
of 7 years after the date of filing.

SECTION 6 — TECHNOLOGY AND DATA GOVERNANCE (NEW)

6.1 Automated Monitoring Requirements

6.1.1 A CMI shall deploy automated monitoring systems that are:
(a) Capable of processing transactions in real-time or near-real-time;
(b) Configurable to incorporate updated risk indicators and typologies;
(c) Subject to regular model validation and performance assessment.

6.2 Data Quality

6.2.1 A CMI shall establish and maintain data quality standards for all
customer and transaction data used in CDD and monitoring processes.
